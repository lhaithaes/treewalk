Attenuation

Attenuation tanks, found under these car park spaces, are an effective water management solution and an important element of a sustainable drainage system. These large containers store excess rainwater from groundwater absorption. Usually, during periods of heavy rainfall, impermeable surfaces like buildings, roads, and car parks, mean that rainwater stays overground and inundates waterways. However, the permeable gravel surface and attenuation tanks allow water to be quickly absorbed and stored underground. The rainwater is then slowly discharged at a controlled rate into the water course.

Flood Management

Mill Close is a high flood risk zone. At the convergence point of the River Don and River Rother, water levels can quickly exceed normal height during period’s heavy rainfall. Hard surfaces further increase flood risk, with flooding having consequences for road traffic and surrounding neighbourhoods.

Attenuation tanks and tarmac alternative permeable surfaces greatly lower the level of flood risk. This is important for the safeguarding of homes and businesses and is a necessary consideration in urban development. Furthermore, flood management is an important aspect of climate change adaptation, with severe rainfall events becoming more intense and frequent in the UK as a result of atmospheric warming.

About Tree Walk

The vision of AESSEAL – and the vision of the Betterworld.Solutions project – is to find ways of making industry and the environment live in harmony. In parallel with a ‘factory of the future’ plan at the AESSEAL® Global Headquarters in Rotherham, England. AESSEAL® is developing ‘Tree Walk’ as an educational and public information resource.

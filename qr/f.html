Pond

The pond you see beside you is a balancing pond and forms part of the AESSEAL Wetlands wildlife sanctuary originally opened in 2005.

The pond is filled with surface water runoff from over sections of the building roof and carpark totalling over 2,000m2. The water is filtered through swales containing grasses, reeds and other aquatic plants to remove any possible contamination.

Flood Provision

The pond is designed to store water within the site only releasing it from site slowly. During storms or extreme rainfall events, the pond can raise its level by as much as 500mm above its outfall before overflowing. This avoids a large proportion of rainfall leaving site, reducing strain on public sewers reducing flash floor risks. In turn it removes the sites impact on the peak flow of the nearby rivers Rother and Don not only protecting the site but also the local communities downstream. Such provisions will become more important as the effects of climate change increase and rainfall patterns become more unpredictable.

Habitat Creation

Bodies of water such as this balancing pond provide benefit to both flora and fauna enhancing the habitat availability for aquatic species and intern insects and birds. The pond has become well established over the last 20 years since its original creation, forming its own micro ecosystem.

The pond provides a source of water during dry weather conditions as well as a holding back water during heavy rainfall. Overtime the pond has attracted both invertebrate and vertebrates, you will often see dragonflies, damselflies, pond skaters and water boatmen from spring through to autumn. This intern attracts other visitors such as frogs, mallards and herons.

About Tree Walk

The vision of AESSEAL – and the vision of the Betterworld.Solutions project – is to find ways of making industry and the environment live in harmony. In parallel with a ‘factory of the future’ plan at the AESSEAL® Global Headquarters in Rotherham, England. AESSEAL® is developing ‘Tree Walk’ as an educational and public information resource.

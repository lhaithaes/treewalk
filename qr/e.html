Sustainable Drainage Systems

Sustainable drainage systems (SuDS) contribute towards the sustainable development of urban areas, by balancing the opportunities and challenges that come with urban development and the communities that live there. 

 SuDS manage three main aspects: water quantity (flooding), water quality (pollution), and biodiversity (wildlife), all of which help to improve the urban environment.

Photo by Jessica Copeland AESSEAL

The Swales

Water can be further led to a retention or balancing pond, another component of a sustainable drainage system. A balancing pond allows water that runs off hard surfaces, such as roofs and roads, to be stored before being released into the waterway.

Swales are ideal for industrial sites and are preferable to conventional pipework as pollution incidents are easily visible. They also have ecological benefits as they resemble natural drainage ditches, and can support many different types of tall grasses and shrubby vegetation.

Balancing pond

Balancing ponds have important benefits for controlling river flow and preventing flooding. These features reduce the impact on communities downstream following large rainfall events. A long retention time also promotes pollutant removal through sedimentation and biological uptake of excess nutrients, contributing towards water quality improvement.

Balancing ponds can also be used to create a wetland environment to support local freshwater species. Overall, balancing ponds can help mitigate the impacts of industrial development, such as harm to wildlife or contamination of drinking water.

About Tree Walk

The vision of AESSEAL – and the vision of the Betterworld.Solutions project – is to find ways of making industry and the environment live in harmony. In parallel with a ‘factory of the future’ plan at the AESSEAL® Global Headquarters in Rotherham, England. AESSEAL® is developing ‘Tree Walk’ as an educational and public information resource.

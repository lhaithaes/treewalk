Meadows

Meadows can transform urban spaces with the help of native flowers and plants, bringing wildlife-rich habitats and pollinator havens. The vibrant colours and amazing smells of meadows are there to be enjoyed and are just one way we can appreciate the natural world.

Reclaiming nature

Since the industrial revolution, the UK has lost 97% of its wildflower meadows. They are extremely diverse habitats with an abundance of floral resources which are a haven for honey bees. This provides lots of opportunities for bees to cross-pollinate plants and also collect pollen to take back to the hive. By supporting pollinators, alongside other insects, meadows also encourage birds, bats, and hedgehogs, to visit. This makes meadows important for the entire ecological community.

Wildflower species identified in this meadow include black-eyed Susan, cornflower, sweet Sophie, and the common poppy, among others. Cornflowers, most commonly an intense blue colour, are native to Europe. However, this species has become endangered due to agricultural intensification, in particular, the over-use of herbicides and nitrogen fertiliser. Cultivating cornflowers is highly appreciated by bees, as their nectar is very sweet, and their seeds are one of the favourite foods of goldfinches.

Underground secrets

Wildflowers are mostly perennial plants, meaning they live for several years. Even though these plants bloom over the spring and summer, and die back in the winter, perennials return in the spring from their roots which lie dormant in the soil. Perennials often have deep, extensive root systems that allow them to out-compete other types of plants, such as weeds.

Perennial root systems also have benefits to the soil structure and prevent the soil from being eroded if the land becomes waterlogged. Their root properties are also efficient at dissolved nitrogen uptake – preventing nitrogen contamination of ground and surface water.

About Tree Walk

The vision of AESSEAL – and the vision of the Betterworld.Solutions project – is to find ways of making industry and the environment live in harmony. In parallel with a ‘factory of the future’ plan at the AESSEAL® Global Headquarters in Rotherham, England. AESSEAL® is developing ‘Tree Walk’ as an educational and public information resource.
